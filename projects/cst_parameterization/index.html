CST Parameterization Capabilites Implemented into NSU2D and NSU3D

This project was funded by Dassault and involved implementing an augmented CST parameterization capability into the NSU2D and NSU3D codes. This augmentation included in airfoil flaps, and thickness constraints implemented in the CST parameterization, enforced at the front and rear spars and the maximum thickness location. Optimization of an RAE airfoil in transonic viscous flow was successful using L-BFGS:

We can see the norm of the sensitivity vector decrease by oneand a half orders of magnitude, and the objective function itself is halved. As before this design optimization terminated as we had capped the number of design cycles at 20, and itâ€™s possible we could have seen further decreases with morecycles as L-BFGS creates a better model of the design space. The right plot, showing the baseline airfoil in black and the optimized one in red, demonstrates the contrast between the two shapes. The thickness does not change much, but the nose flap angles slightly downwards and gets padded slightly on the bottom and decreases the thickness on top, while the rear sees the flap get angled slightly up and the airfoil gets slightly thinner

We can see that as a result of this design optimization the maximum Mach number increases as a very localiized phenomenon and that over all the shock is weaker and occurs further back along the airfoil. Furthermore, the supersonic region is greatly reduced in area.
